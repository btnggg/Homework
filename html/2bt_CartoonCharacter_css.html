Winnie the Pooh

Winnie the Pooh is a character based on A. A. Milne's Winnie-the-Pooh stories. The Disney media franchise commenced in 1966 with the theatrical release of the short Winnie the Pooh and the Honey Tree.

About Winnie the Pooh

  1. The Pooh stories have broken many book records –even in foreign languages.
  2. The first Winnie the Pooh book, simply named Winnie the Pooh, was published in October 1926. Now We Are Six followed in 1927 and House at Pooh Corner was published in 1928.
  3. Winnie the Pooh is also called Pooh, or Pooh Bear, but never, ever, just Winnie.
  4. Pooh and his friends live in the Hundred Acre Wood where they enjoy friendship, warmth and compassion as well as adventure!
  5. Pooh’s closest friends include Tigger, Piglet and Eyeore.
  6. Winnie the Pooh books and videos have been translated in 29 languages.
  7. Winnie the Pooh feature films:
    • Pooh’s Heffalump Movie (2005)
    • Piglet’s Big Movie (2003) The Tigger Movie (2000)

Pooh's friends

  • Tigger is a fictional tiger character originally introduced in A. A. Milne's book The House at Pooh Corner. Like other Pooh characters, Tigger is based on one of Christopher Robin Milne's stuffed animals. Nowadays he is also widely recognized as reinterpreted by the Disney studios, with distinctive orange and black stripes, large eyes, a long chin, a springy tail, and (the one detail originating from A. A. Milne) his love of bouncing. As he says himself, "Bouncing is what Tiggers do best."
  • Piglet is a fictional character from A. A. Milne's Winnie‑the‑Pooh books. Piglet is Winnie‑the‑Pooh's closest friend amongst all the toys/animals featured in the stories. Despite the fact that he is a "Very Small Animal" of a generally timid disposition, he tries to be brave and on occasion conquers his fears.
  • Eeyore is a character in the Winnie-the-Pooh books by A. A. Milne. He is generally characterized as a pessimistic, gloomy, depressed, anhedonic, old grey stuffed donkey who is a friend of the title character, Winnie-the-Pooh.
